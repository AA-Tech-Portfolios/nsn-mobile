# Alpha Tester Feedback Checklist

Use this during or after a short NSN alpha walkthrough. Keep notes plain and specific. One-sentence answers are enough.

## Before You Start

- What device did you use?
- Were you on web, iOS, Android, or another setup?
- Did anything fail to load?
- Did the app feel clearly marked as an early alpha prototype?

## Onboarding Clarity

- Did you understand what NSN is for?
- Did onboarding feel too long, too short, or about right?
- Were any questions too personal too early?
- Did the 18+ wording make sense?
- Did the local-area question make sense?
- Did the final review step help?

## Navigation Clarity

- Could you tell where to go next?
- Were the main tabs clear?
- Did any back button or close button surprise you?
- Did menus feel easy to leave?
- Did you ever feel stuck?

## Home And Event Discovery

- Could you tell what Home was showing?
- Did the local meetup cards make sense?
- Did search feel useful?
- Did filters make the list easier or harder?
- Did Day/Night mode make sense?
- Did Compact, Comfortable, or Spacious layout choices feel useful?
- Was anything visually too busy?

## Meetup Expectations

- Before joining, did you understand the meetup plan?
- Was the meeting point clear enough for a prototype?
- Did group size and vibe expectations feel clear?
- Did weather or indoor/outdoor wording help?
- Did any event feel too vague?
- Did any event feel too high-pressure?

## RSVP And Join Flow

- Did the join or RSVP wording feel clear?
- Did it seem honest that RSVP is prototype/local-only?
- Would you expect a real host to be notified?
- Was it clear that attendance is not production-tracked?
- Did leaving or changing your mind feel okay?

## Chats

- Did Chats feel clearly tied to meetups?
- Did chat feel calm or pressured?
- Was it clear this is not production messaging yet?
- Would you know what chat is safe to use for?
- Was any message wording confusing?

## Comfort And Privacy Understanding

- Did comfort settings feel easy to understand?
- Did privacy controls feel reassuring?
- Did photo blur make sense?
- Did profile visibility wording feel clear?
- Did any control promise more than the prototype can really do?
- Did you understand what was local-only?

## Settings And Privacy Usability

- Could you find Settings & Privacy?
- Were the sections named clearly?
- Did expanded sections fit on your screen?
- Were switches, chips, and buttons easy to use?
- Did account pause/delete demo wording feel honest?
- Was anything too hidden?

## Emotional Pressure Or Overwhelm

- Did any screen feel like too much?
- Did any wording make you feel judged or rushed?
- Did joining a meetup feel low-pressure?
- Did profile setup feel manageable?
- Did the app give enough permission to leave, pause, or decide later?

## Confusing Wording

- Which words or labels were unclear?
- Did anything sound too corporate?
- Did anything sound too serious for a prototype?
- Did anything sound too casual for a safety-related area?
- Was any Australian/local wording missing or awkward?

## Unclear Buttons Or Flows

- Which button did you hesitate on?
- Did any button do something different from what you expected?
- Were demo, coming soon, and saved locally states clear?
- Did disabled controls explain enough?
- Did any flow end without enough feedback?

## Mobile Layout And Readability

- Was text readable on your device?
- Did any card, chip, or button feel cramped?
- Did anything overlap?
- Could you scroll comfortably?
- Were important actions too close to the bottom edge?
- Did long labels wrap cleanly?

## Trust And Safety Perception

- Did the app feel honest about safety limits?
- Did report/block-shaped controls feel understandable?
- Did any safety wording feel like a real service when it is not?
- Did verification wording feel prototype-only?
- Would you want more human organiser confirmation before a real meetup?
- What would make you trust NSN more later?

## What Felt Calm Or Helpful?

- What made the app feel easier to use?
- What wording felt reassuring?
- Which screen felt most useful?
- Which control reduced pressure?
- What would you keep as-is?

## What Felt Confusing Or Stressful?

- What caused hesitation?
- What felt too heavy?
- What felt unfinished in a worrying way?
- What did you not understand?
- What would you change first?

## What Would Stop You From Using This?

- What would make you leave the app?
- What would stop you from joining a meetup?
- What would make you worry about privacy?
- What would make you worry about safety?
- What would need to be real before you used NSN with strangers?

## Final Notes

- What is the one thing NSN should improve before the next alpha round?
- What is the one thing NSN should not lose?
- Would you be comfortable testing again after changes?
