# NSN Alpha Tester Guide

North Shore Nights (NSN) is an early controlled alpha prototype for calm, low-pressure meetups around Sydney's North Shore. It is being tested with trusted people so we can learn whether the flow feels clear, useful, respectful, and local.

This is not a public launch. The app is still a prototype, and several important systems are local-only, mocked, or not production-ready yet.

## What NSN Currently Is

NSN is a product exploration for people who want easier ways to find small local plans without the pressure of big social apps. The current prototype focuses on:

- gentle onboarding
- local meetup browsing
- clear expectations before joining
- comfort and privacy controls
- small-group social planning ideas
- early chat and meetup screens
- honest safety and prototype labels

The goal of alpha testing is to understand how the experience feels, not to prove that every feature is finished.

## What Works Today

You can currently try:

- onboarding and profile setup
- browsing local meetup cards on Home
- switching Home views, filters, and layout density
- opening meetup details
- reading expectations, meeting points, comfort notes, and weather-aware copy
- using local join/leave-style RSVP states
- viewing Meetups and Chats prototype screens
- changing profile, privacy, comfort, accessibility, language, and notification preferences
- trying Soft Exit, block/report-shaped controls, and other safety-adjacent prototype surfaces

Most of this is designed to run locally as a realistic app-shaped prototype.

## What Is Still Prototype Or Local-Only

Please do not rely on NSN yet for real-world safety, identity, attendance, or account management. These areas are not production-ready:

- real authentication and account lifecycle
- real verification or identity checks
- production moderation, report review, or operator escalation
- emergency tools, trusted contacts, check-in timers, or live safety response
- backend RSVP persistence, waitlists, host approval, or attendance tracking
- live server-backed group chat
- production push notifications
- real map, route, transport, weather, or venue data guarantees

Some actions may save locally on your device or browser. Some buttons are intentionally demo-only or coming soon. Local data may reset.

## Onboarding

Please move through onboarding as if you were a new tester hearing about NSN for the first time.

Notice whether:

- the purpose of NSN is clear
- the 18+ and local-area wording makes sense
- comfort preferences feel easy to answer
- privacy choices are understandable
- the review step gives enough confidence before entering the app

There is no need to make your profile perfect. Use realistic answers if you are comfortable, or test with broad, non-identifying information.

## Browsing Local Meetups

Home is where you can explore local meetup ideas. Try browsing by:

- nearby or local-area relevance
- day and night modes
- small groups
- weather-safe options
- search and filters
- compact, comfortable, and spacious layouts

Please pay attention to whether each meetup explains enough before you would consider joining. We are especially interested in whether the meetup cards feel calm, readable, and locally believable.

## RSVP Expectations

The current RSVP and join states are prototype-only. They may be saved locally, but they are not backed by an authoritative RSVP system.

For alpha testing:

- treat RSVP actions as a way to test the flow
- do not assume your RSVP has reached a real host
- do not assume attendance is tracked
- wait for separate organiser confirmation before any real meetup plan

Useful feedback includes whether the RSVP wording feels too strong, too vague, or just right for an early, low-pressure meetup app.

## Chats

Chats are currently prototype screens, not a production messaging system.

Please test whether the chat area feels:

- easy to understand
- appropriately calm
- clearly tied to a meetup
- low-pressure for shy or first-time users
- honest about its alpha status

Do not use chat for urgent, private, safety-critical, or real coordination needs during this alpha.

## Profile And Privacy Settings

Try the Profile, User Preferences, and Settings & Privacy areas. These include controls for visibility, photo blur, local area display, comfort preferences, contact preferences, language, accessibility, appearance, and account-style actions.

Please remember:

- privacy controls are prototype UI and local state
- profile visibility is not backend-enforced yet
- account pause/delete actions are not real production account lifecycle tools
- verification and trust indicators are not real verified identity checks

We want to know whether the controls are understandable and reassuring before they become more serious systems.

## Comfort Controls

NSN is meant to support different levels of social comfort. Please try settings related to:

- meeting comfort
- group-size comfort
- communication preference
- social energy
- profile visibility
- saved comfort places
- soft exit or leaving-style flows

Useful feedback is not only "does it work?" but also "does it reduce pressure?" If a label feels awkward, heavy, or unclear, please tell us.

## What Not To Rely On Yet

During this controlled alpha, please do not treat NSN as:

- a real safety service
- a verified community
- a production account system
- a final RSVP or attendance record
- a live emergency or escalation tool
- a fully moderated platform
- a reliable source of live venue, weather, transport, or route information

If a real meetup is being tested, follow the separate organiser instructions outside the app.

## How To Give Useful Feedback

The most helpful feedback is specific and plain. Short notes are completely fine.

Good feedback sounds like:

- "I understood this screen, but the button made me nervous."
- "This felt calm, but I wasn't sure what would happen after joining."
- "The privacy setting sounded serious, but I could not tell if it was real yet."
- "This wording felt too formal for a local Sydney meetup."
- "I would want more reassurance before going to this event."

Please mention:

- what screen you were on
- what you expected to happen
- what actually happened
- what felt calm, confusing, helpful, or stressful
- whether anything would stop you from using NSN again

There is no pressure to be positive. Honest friction is the point of alpha testing.
