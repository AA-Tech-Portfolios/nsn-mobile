# Brand Systems

> North Shore Nights (NSN) is the active app identity for this repository. SoftHello references in this document are future/global context only.

This repo currently contains two related but distinct product identities:

- **SoftHello** is the global release direction.
- **North Shore Nights (NSN)** is the local Sydney/North Shore prototype direction.

The products can share low-pressure meetup principles, trust flows, accessibility thinking,
and safety patterns. They should not accidentally share all visual language or local copy.

## SoftHello

SoftHello is the global, calmer, more broadly welcoming identity. It should feel warm,
human, soft, respectful, and safe without becoming childish or overly decorative.

### Original Concept Anchor

The original SoftHello concept is a soft, global, events-first release direction with a
purple/peach identity and a warm onboarding flow. Its headline promise is:

> Say hello, your way.

The core journey is:

- **Onboarding:** learn about the user in a warm, guided way.
- **Events First:** discover local events that match the user's vibe.
- **Event Details:** see who is coming, the vibe, and what to expect.
- **Intro Chat:** chat in the group at the user's own pace.
- **After the Event:** share feedback, celebrate good vibes, and build trust.

The original concept also introduces:

- age and preferred age range during onboarding.
- local area input with region-aware language.
- first-meetup interests such as coffee, movies, walks, dinner, games, and more.
- comfort levels including Comfort Mode, Warm Up Mode, and Open Mode.
- profile reveal patterns where photos stay blurred until both people are ready.
- post-event feedback focused on comfort, respect, conversation quality, and whether the
  user would meet again.

### Product Position

- Global release name and identity.
- Local events can still exist, but copy should avoid feeling tied only to Sydney or Australia.
- Active NSN location fields should stay local to Sydney's North Shore, NSW, and Australia/Sydney time.
- Suburb/local-area language can be adapted by region rather than hard-coded as Australian-only wording.

### Future Core Features

- **Events first:** browse local events that match your vibe.
- **Comfort and privacy:** blurred profiles, reveal only when ready.
- **Verified and safe:** future human verification, moderation, and safety systems that users can trust once real providers and backend workflows exist.
- **Local matching:** meet people near you, not overseas by default.
- **Trust and feedback:** private community feedback that can support safer operations once moderation exists.

### Future Trust and Safety Pillars

- **Identity verification:** real people only.
- **Location verification:** locally verified members where appropriate.
- **Report and block:** easy reporting tools.
- **Secure evidence:** preserve safety evidence for investigations.
- **Community standards:** respect, always.

These are SoftHello product goals, not current NSN production capabilities. See [`current-state.md`](current-state.md) for what exists now.

### Typography

- Primary family: **Poppins**.
- Headings and titles: Poppins SemiBold.
- Body and paragraph text: Poppins Regular.
- Buttons and labels: Poppins Medium.

### Logo Direction

- Use the overlapping speech-bubble mark for SoftHello.
- Preferred lockups:
  - icon above wordmark for onboarding and splash screens.
  - horizontal icon plus wordmark for headers and web placements.
  - icon-only app mark for small surfaces.
- Avoid placing SoftHello inside NSN dark-blue treatments unless intentionally showing a migration or comparison screen.

### Icon Style

- Rounded, simple line icons.
- Soft, friendly, low-pressure forms.
- Use purple for active states and muted ink/slate for inactive states.
- Avoid sharp, technical, or alarm-heavy iconography unless the flow is explicitly about safety escalation.

### Brand Tone

- **Warm:** welcoming and human.
- **Calm:** low pressure, always.
- **Trustworthy:** safety comes first.
- **Inclusive:** everyone belongs.
- **Genuine:** real connections that matter.

## North Shore Nights

NSN is the local Australian prototype identity. It can be more place-based, night-friendly,
and Sydney/North Shore specific.

### Version 1.0 Concept Anchor

The original NSN v1.0 concept is a dark, iOS-inspired local meetup experience with four
primary screens:

- **Home:** Tonight / This Week discovery, calm category chips, compact event cards, and a
  clear Create a Meetup action.
- **Event Details:** event identity, location, date/time, group size, vibe, activity type,
  meeting point, expectations, and Join Meetup.
- **Group Chat:** scoped meetup chat with a small member count, visible participant messages,
  composer, and disappearing-chat reminder.
- **Profile:** avatar, vibes, about text, saved places, settings, and bottom-tab navigation.

This concept should remain the reference for NSN-specific screens. Later SoftHello screens can
reuse the low-pressure flow, but should translate the visuals into the softer global brand.

### Version 1.1 Concept Anchor

NSN v1.1 keeps the dark local identity and expands the experience into a more complete
weather-aware meetup product:

- **Home (Day and Night):** a Day/Night segmented control, local date and suburb context,
  weather update, category filters, Day Events, Evening Events, and clearer timing-based
  discovery.
- **Event Details:** event sharing and options, indoor/quiet chips, weather update, expectation
  cards, meeting point, and a Join Meetup action with spots remaining.
- **Group Chat:** scoped event chat with member count, call/options affordances, arrival
  updates, composer, and disappearing-chat reminder.
- **Profile:** avatar, vibes, about text, meetup/chat/event shortcuts, saved places, and
  settings/privacy access.

The v1.1 concept also introduces feature cards that are central to the NSN product:

- **Day vs Night:** help users find the right vibe at the right time.
- **Noise Level Guide:** separate quiet, balanced, and lively venue energy from social pressure.
- **Weather Adaptive:** notify users when the weather changes and suggest indoor alternatives.
- **Indoor Alternatives:** provide a gentle fallback plan, such as switching an outdoor picnic
  to an indoor bowling/movie meetup when rain is expected.

### Product Position

- Local prototype name and identity.
- Designed around Sydney's North Shore and nearby Australian localities.
- Suburb, local area, venue, and Australian timezone language is appropriate here.
- Works as a local proof-of-concept before SoftHello becomes the broader product system.

- Weather-aware, suburb-aware, and venue-aware.
- Friendly but less globally polished than SoftHello.
- Useful for testing local meetup mechanics before globalizing copy and settings.

## Shared Product Principles

Both identities should preserve:

- Low-pressure social discovery.
- Clear expectations before joining a meetup.
- Safety, reporting, blocking, and verification flows.
- Comfort Mode and Open Mode privacy controls.
- Noise Level Guide that separates venue sound from social talking pressure.
- Accessibility settings and screen reader support.
- Gentle exits, arrival updates, and respectful cancellation flows.

Current implementation status for these shared principles belongs in [`current-state.md`](current-state.md), not in this brand document.

## Implementation Rules

- Do not mix SoftHello purple/peach branding into NSN screens unless the screen is explicitly part of the global rebrand direction.
- Do not use Australian-only terms such as suburb as the only location language in SoftHello global flows.
- Keep local Australian locality matching in NSN-specific or region-aware modules.
- When adding new screens, decide whether the screen belongs to SoftHello global, NSN local, or both before styling it.
- If a shared feature has region-specific copy, keep the feature logic shared and localize the language at the copy/settings layer.
